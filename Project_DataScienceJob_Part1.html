What is "Data Scientist"?

Python, Jupyter Notebook

Introduction

A year ago, I graduated from one of most popular majors, statistics. Yeah, thanks to the trending of Big Data and AI.

Gradually, there are more and more data science related positions sprang up, such as data engineer, machine learning engineer, business analyst, ..., etc. People who work on data science think that they are data scientists. Really? That's an interesting question and there are tons of article have talked about these.

To know better about the difference, this project collected about 2000 job descriptions by searching position keywords, including data scientist, data analyst, machine learning engineer, business analyst, and data engineer, in the indeed.com and summarized the difference about those position. I usually used data science to explain the data. This time, I would like to use data to explain the data science.

Data

There are 1801 job post in total. The table shows the number of job descriptions in each searched title. The graph shows the distribtion of those titles in States. THe graph shows the percentage of jobs in each state. Also, includes the percentage of jobs for these five positions in each state.


Most of jobs come from California. In NY, Data Analyst dominated that state because of the financial industry.

Comparison

According to the NLP from job description, Data Analyst focuses more on SQL, Tableau than Data Scientist. R and Python are the essential skills for the Data Scientist. People use Python for many reasons, including development, algorithm, and models. On the other hand, R helps data scientist to analyze data with statistical methods, models, and manulputaion.As a person graduated from statistics, I would say the weights of imporatnce between Python and R are equal.
